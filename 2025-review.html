Our Story

Founded in April 2025, RAINES is a community for Korean professionals working and researching in the Robotics and AI fields throughout the New England area. Through research seminars, career seminars, and networking events, we aim to expand our members' professional networks and foster innovation and growth in the field.

2025 Timeline

April 28, 2025
RAINES Launching Event: AI-Powered Robotics and Autonomous Driving Seminar

Location: CIC Cambridge

Speakers: Dr. Haewon Park (MIT Media Lab), Prof. Yoon Kim (MIT EECS)

Attendees: 49 total (35 from Industry, 14 from Academia)

This seminar, hosted by the Korean Consulate in Boston, brought together experts and enthusiasts to explore the future of AI-powered robotics and autonomous driving. Dr. Haewon Park shared insights on social robots, while Prof. Yoon Kim discussed large language models, sparking engaging conversations on emerging technologies. The event fostered US-Korea collaboration, offering a platform for networking, knowledge sharing, and building new connections in the Boston tech community.

August 21, 2025
RAINES Summer Seminar: Recent Advances in AI Problem Solving

Location: Korean Consulate General in Boston

Speakers: Dr. Jungkoo Kang (IBM Research), Prof. Junehyuk Jung (Brown University & Google DeepMind)

Attendees: 55 total (sold out capacity)

On August 21st, we successfully concluded the K-RAINES Summer Seminar at the newly renovated Boston Consulate General! With a sold-out capacity of 55 attendees, this seminar was conducted with tremendous enthusiasm. Dr. Jungkoo Kang from IBM Research shared insights on LLM-based problem solving and workflow generation, while Prof. Junehyuk Jung from Brown University & Google DeepMind presented research on automated mathematical reasoning. We had meaningful time with insights from two speakers at the forefront of AI research, along with networking and dinner with participants. Thanks to the various prizes provided by the Boston Consulate General, we also enjoyed a fun raffle time, and most participants were able to take home a gift. We thank the Consulate General of the Republic of Korea in Boston, Korea Evaluation Institute of Industrial Technology (KEIT), Rainbow Robotics, and all attendees who joined us for this seminar!

November 17, 2025
K-RAINES Fall Event: The Physical AI Era: Korea-US New Technology Cooperation

Location: CIC Venture Café (1 Broadway, Cambridge)

Date & Time: November 17, 2025 (Monday) 5:30 PM - 8:00 PM

Speakers: CEO Lee Gil-woo (Zordi), Prof. Benjamin Marlin (University of Massachusetts Amherst), Prof. Kim Chang-ik (KAIST), Advisor HK Kwon (권혁민, Carbon Black), Director Lee Hoon-bae (이훈배, TIPA)

Attendees: 48 people attended

On November 17th, K-RAINES successfully hosted its fall networking event, "The Physical AI Era: Korea-US New Technology Cooperation," at CIC Venture Café in Cambridge. This special event, sponsored by the Consulate General of the Republic of Korea in Boston, Overseas Koreans Agency, TIPA (Korea Technology and Information Promotion Agency for SMEs), Carbon Black, and Carbon Six, brought together 48 attendees to explore the exciting frontier of Physical AI. The event began with sponsor speeches, starting with Deputy Consul General Kwak Jung-yeol (곽정열) from the Consulate General of the Republic of Korea in Boston, followed by Director Lee Hoon-bae (이훈배) from TIPA and Advisor HK Kwon (권혁민) representing Carbon Black. Three distinguished speakers then shared their insights: CEO Lee Gil-woo from Zordi, Prof. Benjamin Marlin from University of Massachusetts Amherst, and Prof. Kim Chang-ik from KAIST. The event featured engaging presentations and Q&A sessions, followed by a panel discussion with all speakers, and concluded with networking and dinner, fostering meaningful connections and collaboration between Korea and the US in advancing AI technologies. This event marked a wonderful conclusion to the year, bringing together the community to share knowledge and build lasting relationships in the Boston tech ecosystem.
